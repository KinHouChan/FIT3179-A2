Across Different Life Stages: How Do Death Rates Differ?

Top 10 Death Causes by Gender: A Comparison Across Two Years

Across Malaysia, death rates paint a concerning picture, with the Maternal and Perinatal stages consistently recording the highest numbers. Limited access to quality prenatal care, childbirth complications, and delayed medical interventions for newborns are significant contributors. In states like Labuan and Putrajaya, these issues are more pronounced, reflecting disparities in healthcare infrastructure.

Improving access to early intervention and maternal care could lead to significant reductions in mortality rates, especially in these critical life stages, where timely healthcare can make a life-saving difference.

Between 2019 and 2021, stark contrasts in death rates emerged between genders, with ischaemic heart disease and COVID-19 hitting man hardest. The surge in COVID-19 fatalities further highlights these vulnerabilities. Cerebrovascular and hypertensive diseases reflect aging and lifestyle risks that both men and women face. Breast cancer remains a concern for women, emphasizing the need for targeted healthcare.

Higher male mortality from transport accidents also strongly points to riskier behaviors, underscoring the urgent need for more tailored public health measures and interventions.
